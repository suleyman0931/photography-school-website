Introduction to Photography

Photography is the art, science, and practice of creating images by recording light. It is a versatile medium that can be used for a wide variety of purposes, including capturing memories, documenting events, and expressing creativity.

An introduction to photography course will teach you the basics of photography, including:

• Camera basics: How to choose and use a camera, including understanding the different types of cameras, lenses, and settings.
• Composition: The art of arranging elements in a photograph to create a pleasing image.
• Lighting: How to use natural and artificial light to create different effects in your photographs.
• Exposure: How to control the amount of light that enters the camera to create a properly exposed image.

You will also learn about the different genres of photography, such as portraiture, landscape photography, and street photography.

An introduction to photography course is a great way to learn the basics of photography and start taking better pictures. It is also a good foundation for those who want to pursue photography as a hobby or career.

What you will learn in an introduction to photography course:

  • The basics of camera operation, including aperture, shutter speed, and ISO.
  • How to compose a photograph using the rule of thirds, leading lines, and other composition techniques.
  • How to use natural and artificial light to create different effects in your photographs.
  • How to control exposure to create a properly exposed image.
  • The different genres of photography, such as portraiture, landscape photography, and street photography.

By the end of an introduction to photography course, you will be able to:

  • Choose and use a camera to take better pictures
  • Compose photographs that are pleasing to the eye.
  • Use lighting to create different effects in your photographs.
  • Control exposure to create properly exposed images.
  • Identify the different genres of photography and understand their unique characteristics.





Welcome to the world of digital photography!

This course is designed to help you gain a better understanding of photography by exploring camera functions and the elements of composition while putting theory into practice by taking your own spectacular shots. You'll learn how to display your work for exhibitions and develop skills important for a career as a photographer.

Unit 1

"Taking The First Shot," you'll be introduced to the digital age of photography and learn how to take your first shot. You'll explore the basics of digital photography and learn how to use your camera to capture stunning images.

Unit 2

"Moving Into Manual," you'll learn how to use manual settings on your camera to take control of your photography. You'll explore the many ways basic camera functions are affected by certain manual modes and how you can use them to create exactly the right setting for your shot.

Unit 3

"A Snapshot of History," you'll take a look back at the history of photography and learn about the innovators, scientists, and creators who came before us in the field. You'll gain an understanding of emerging trends still widely seen today and learn how to take your own visions to the next level.

Unit 4

The Nature of Light," you'll learn about the elements of light and control and how they work together to create certain effects. You'll gain a practical awareness of where light comes from, how it is characterized, and what it can accomplish in a picture.

Unit 5

"Creative Composition," you'll learn visual organizational strategies and how your camera can help you apply them. You'll gain the control and understanding you need to find just the right composition, no matter where or what you are shooting.
